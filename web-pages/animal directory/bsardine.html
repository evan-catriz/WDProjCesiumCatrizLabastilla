Status: Endangered (E)
Appearance: Similar to saltwater sardines but more delicate and slender, with a laterally compressed body, a single triangular dorsal fin, forked tail, and tough belly scales (scutes).

Locations:
Taal Lake, Batangas

Numbers left: Annual harvest decreased at least 50% over a 10-year period.
Habitat: Endemic to Taal Lake (freshwater lake).
Size: 10 to 15 centimeters (4 to 6 inches) in total length.
Diet: primarily eats zooplankton, like copepods and cladocerans.
Lifespan: 3-5 years

Fun fact: Tawilis (scientific name Sardinella tawilis) adapted from saltwater to freshwater after Taal Lake was cut off from the sea, making it a biological marvel.
