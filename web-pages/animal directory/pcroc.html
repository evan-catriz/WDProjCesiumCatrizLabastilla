Status: Critically endangered (CR) Appearance: Typically brown or gray with black markings as adults, featuring a broad snout and thick bony plates on its back, while juveniles are golden-brown with dark stripes and lighter undersides.

Locations:
Northern Sierra Madre, Luzon
Dalupiri islands, Cagayan valley
Ligawan Marsh, Mindanao

Numbers left: ~100 adults.
Habitat: Freshwater habitats such as rivers, ponds, marshes, small lakes.
Size: males reach up to 10 feet (3 meters) and females are generally smaller, though most individuals are around 6-7 feet long, weighing up to 90-190 kg.
Diet: invertebrates like snails, shrimps, and dragonflies (juveniles) to larger prey such as fish, snakes, water birds, pigs, and even dogs (adults).
Lifespan: May live as long as 80 years in the wild.
Fun fact: They swallow stones (gastroliths) to adjust their weight, helping them sink or float in the water.
