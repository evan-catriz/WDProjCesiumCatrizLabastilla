Status: Critically Endangered (CR)
Appearance: shaggy, brown-and-cream feathered crest, dark brown back, white underside, piercing blue-gray eyes, and a massive bluish-gray beak, with powerful yellow legs and dark talons.

Locations:
Samar, Luzon
Leyte, Visayas
Mount Apo, Mindanao

Numbers left: ~800 individuals in the wild.
Habitat: Old-growth tropical rainforests, specifically dipterocarp forests and montane forests, typically nesting in giant hardwood trees within steep and rugged mountain areas.
Size: length of 86–102 cm (2 ft 10 in–3 ft 4 in) and a wingspan of up to 220 cm (7 ft 3 in).
Diet: medium-sized mammals like Philippine flying lemurs, palm civets, and flying squirrels, but they're opportunistic apex predators, also eating monkeys, snakes, monitor lizards, rats, and even other birds (like hornbills or raptors).
Lifespan: 30-60 years in the wild.

Fun fact: Philippine Eagles mate for life and perform elaborate aerial courtship displays, and the female typically lays only one egg every couple of years.
