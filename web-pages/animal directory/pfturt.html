Philippine Forest Turtle

Status: Critically endangered (CR)
Appearance: brownish-black with rough, tuberculate skin, known for a distinctive white or yellow "bowtie" stripe across its head, especially prominent in juveniles, fading with age, along with sometimes pink/orange spots on the head and jaw. Its large, dark carapace (shell) can have jagged edges, and its webbed limbs have large scales and claws, with a darker front.

Locations:
Palawan, MIMAROPA

Numbers left: 3,000-4,000 in the wild.
Habitat: Cool, clear streams, swamps, and riparian lowland forests of Palawan, Philippines Size: Typically reaching about 21 cm (8.3 in), but large individuals can grow to over 30 cm (12 in) in carapace length and weigh up to 3.5 kg (7.7 lbs).
Diet: Aquatic plants, figs, small fish, crustaceans, insects, and mollusks.
Lifespan: Appear to exhibit long lifespans.
Fun fact: "Bowtie" Mark: It has a unique light-colored band on its head that looks like a bowtie, which fades with age.
