Status: Endangered (E)
Appearance: small and short-legged with a deep brown coat marked by distinctive, lighter beige spots forming an "A" shape on its back and sides, plus creamy white underparts, chin, and lower lip.

Locations:
Panay islands, Visayas
Negros, Visayas

Numbers left: ~700 individuals in the wild.
Habitat: tropical moist forests, dense shrublands.
Size: 60-80 cm (2-2.6 ft) tall at the shoulder, with adults weighing roughly 40-60 kg (88-132 lbs) and measuring around 125-130 cm (49-51 in) long.
Diet: various grasses, young leaves, and buds from plants found in its Visayan island rainforest habitat, often preferring succulent new growth in clearings after fires, and also consuming fallen fruits and berries.
Lifespan: 18-20 years in captivity.
Fun fact: Males make a distinct "roar," like a dog's bark from afar, to challenge rivals and attract females.
