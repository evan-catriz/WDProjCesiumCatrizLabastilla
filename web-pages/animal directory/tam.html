Status: Critically endangered (CR)
Appearance: Small, stocky, dark brown to grayish, has distinctive short, stout, V-shaped horns that point backward, with lighter "eyebrow" markings above the eyes, white markings on the inner legs, and a dark dorsal line.

Locations:
Mindoro Island

Numbers left: 300-400 individuals in the wild.
Habitat: Mixed forests and grasslands, thick brush, near open-canopied glades.
Size: About 100-105 cm (around 3.3 ft) at the shoulder, with a body length of about 2.2 meters, and weighing roughly 180-300 kg (400-660 lbs).
Diet: Grasses, bamboo shoots, and other vegetation.
Lifespan: 20-25 years in the wild.

Fun fact: Once diurnal (day-active), tamaraws became mostly nocturnal (night-active) to evade humans, making them hard to spot.
