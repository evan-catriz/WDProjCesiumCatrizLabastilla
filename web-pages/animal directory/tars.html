Philippine Forest Turtle

Status: Near Threatened (NT)
Appearance: huge, round eyes that can't move, a soft gray-brown body, large bat-like ears, and a long, thin tail with a tuft at the end.

Locations:
Bohol
Samar
Leyte

Numbers left: 5,000-10,000 individuals left.
Habitat: Tropical rainforests of Southern Philippines.
Size: Body length of 85–160 mm (3.5–6 inches) and weighing around 100–160 grams (3.5–5.3 oz), plus a long, tufted tail that's roughly twice its body length.
Diet: Live insects like crickets, grasshoppers, beetles, and cockroaches, but they also opportunistically eat spiders, lizards, frogs, small snakes, and even baby birds or bats.
Lifespan: typically 10-12 years in the wild, but can reach up to 13-20 years in captivity.

Fun fact: Each eyeball is as large as its brain, and they can't rotate. To compensate, they can swivel their heads almost 180 degrees.
