Philppine Eagle Foundation

Dedicated to saving out endangered national bird and its rainforest habitat through research, community engagement, and aiming for sustainable use of forests.

Katala Foundation

Focused on conserving highly threatened endemic wildlife, primarily in Palawan, using a community-based, ecosystemic approach, with the critically endangered Philippine Cockatoo (locally called "Katala") as its flagship species.

Mabuwaya Foundation

A Philippine conservation organization focused on protecting the critically endangered Philippine crocodile and its habitat, primarily in Northern Luzon's Sierra Madre region, using a community-based approach that involves local education (CEPA), habitat restoration, and partnerships with communities and government agencies like the DENR to involve people in conservation efforts.

Talarak Foundation

focused on saving critically endangered, endemic West Visayan wildlife through captive breeding, habitat restoration, research, and community education.

Jane Goodall Institute

Founded by Dr. Jane Goodall in 1977, dedicated to protecting wildlife (especially great apes), empowering communities through science and technology, improving human and animal well-being (One Health), and fostering youth leadership via its Roots & Shoots program, all rooted in compassionate action for a better planet.

Wildlife Conservation Society

A global non-profit organization, founded in 1895, dedicated to saving wildlife and wild places through science, action, and education, operating in over 50 countries with programs focused on protecting biodiversity hotspots.

Defenders of Wildlife

A leading U.S. non-profit organization founded in 1947, dedicated to protecting and restoring native animals and plants and their natural habitats across North America through science, policy, advocacy, and legal action.

Rainforest Alliance

A global non-profit organization that works to create a sustainable world by protecting nature and improving the lives of farmers and forest communities through rigorous standards for sustainable agriculture, forestry, and tourism, using its iconic frog seal to certify products like coffee and cocoa that meet environmental, social, and economic criteria.

International Crane Foundation

A global non-profit dedicated to conserving the world's 15 crane species and their vital wetland habitats through research, education, captive breeding, reintroduction, and community-based programs.

Why should we support these organizations?

Did you know?
We are currently living through the world's sixth mass extinction. This is a rare event wherein at least 75% of all living species on Earth die out within a relatively short period of time. Factors such as climate change have only further contributed to the rapid decline of our ecosystems. Soon enough, it will not only be the plant and animal species suffering, but humanity as well.
